Neapolitan Pizza

What is Neapolitan Pizza?

Neapolitan pizza is pizza made in the style that originated in Naples, Italy. Like any famous dish, there many variations on what it means to be “authentic.” Most Neapolitan pizzas are baked in a wood fired pizza oven that’s around 800 to 1000 degrees Fahrenheit. The crust is typically blackened by the fire, with puffed edges and very thin towards the middle.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • Flour – 620g
  • Water (room temperature)
  • Salt (fine) – 14g
  • Yeast (dried or fresh) – around 0.2g to 0.5g

For the Toppings

  • 300g tin of plum tomatoes
  • Salt – sprinkling of table salt or sea salt
  • Pepper – freshly ground black pepper
  • Mozzarella – 2 x 125g bags of fresh Mozzarella balls
  • Olive Oil – a few glugs
  • Basil

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough, mix all the ingredients into a shaggy mass in a large bowl, cover the bowl and leave to rest for 1 hour
  2. For the Tomatoe sauce, blend a tin of quality plum tomatoes into a smooth sauce. Cook the sauce until it reaches the desired thickness, season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. for shaping/cooking, ensure that you baking stone/tray is inside your oven.
  4. Get your oven up to temperature.For a regular domestic oven, as hot as it will get (around 260 Celsius or 500 Fahrenheit).
  5. Dust a dough ball with plenty of flour, in the bowl, press down from the centre of the dough towards the edges.
  6. Keep turning the dough as you work the air towards the edges. You should start forming a small pizza shape at this stage.
  7. Remove from the bowl and place on the counter, stretch the dough, repeat the stretch
  8. Top the base with around 2 soup spoons of tomato sauce
  9. Spread about a handful of Mozzarella
  10. Slide the pizza onto a peel
  11. Load straight onto baking stone/tray inside oven. For a regular domestic oven, cook for around 6 – 8 minutes, turning about every 2 – 3 minutes as required.
  12. Remove the pizza from the oven, Grate parmesan on top and add some torn basil. Finish with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkling of black pepper if you like. And your ready

Buon appetito!
